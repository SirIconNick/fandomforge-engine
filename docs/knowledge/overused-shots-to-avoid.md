# Overused Shots to Avoid

Some shots have been used in so many edits they've become invisible. Using them without adding a fresh angle means your edit reads as derivative — even if the rest of it is great.

This list changes over time as trends cycle. This is current as of early 2026. Check if you're unsure; a shot that felt fresh a year ago might be everywhere now.

## Marvel / MCU

**Overused:**
- Tony Stark's snap in Endgame (the "I am Iron Man" sequence)
- Cap "Avengers Assemble" line + assemble wide shot
- The portals opening in Endgame final battle
- Tony's first flight in IM1 with the Black Sabbath reveal
- Cap catching the Mjolnir
- Loki on the throne saying "I..."
- Peter's "Mr. Stark I don't feel so good"
- Tony's death moment ("I love you 3000" / snap aftermath)

**Still works if:**
- You're DIRECTLY calling back to these as thematic anchors
- Using only a new angle (BTS footage, unused coverage)
- Flash-stacked with other deaths in a sacrifice edit

**Use instead (still Marvel, less-worn):**
- Hulk vs. Thanos fight in Infinity War (the arm-break)
- Yondu's funeral ("He may have been your father but he wasn't your daddy" — wait, this is overused too) — OK use the arrow whistle instead
- Vision's death (the stone removal)
- Nat and Clint on Vormir (the pre-sacrifice conversation)
- Wanda's "you took everything from me" to Thanos

## What to do with overused shots

If you MUST use one:

1. **Use it in an unexpected context.** The Itachi poke in an edit about JOY hits different than in a sad edit.
2. **Use a less-common angle from the same scene.** Most overuse is one specific frame. Find the reverse shot.
3. **Speed-ramp it differently.** If everyone uses it slowed down, use it at speed, or vice versa.
4. **Cut into it unexpectedly.** Don't do the cliché "build up to the iconic moment." Jump cut to it cold.
5. **Use only part of the scene.** If everyone uses the 8-second version, use the 2-second partial.

## The "it's 2026 right now" refresh

Because trends shift, check yourself before using a shot:

- Scroll TikTok edits in your theme for 10 minutes
- See what's being used heavily in the top results
- Note what makes you say "I've seen this 50 times"
- Avoid those shots for 6-12 months

Fresh shots are part of craft. The iconic ones don't go away — they come back every few years when they've rested enough.

## When overused is intentional

You CAN lean into an overused shot deliberately:
- As a wink / meta moment
- As a theme-statement shot everyone will recognize
- As the finale everyone is expecting (earn it)

If you use an overused shot as your FINAL shot, it had better be placed masterfully. Otherwise the edit closes on a cliché and viewers forget it.
