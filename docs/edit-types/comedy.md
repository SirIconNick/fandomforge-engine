# Comedy / Meme

Timing-driven humor. Every cut serves the joke. Rules exist to be broken. The AMV community's official guidance on comedy: "few overall guidelines exist for this genre" — intentional rule-breaking is acceptable if it generates laughs.

## Target numbers

| Metric | Target | Range |
|---|---|---|
| Median shot duration | **1.5s** | 0.3 – 5.0s |
| Cuts per minute | **35** | 20 – 50 |
| Beat-sync % | **45%** | 30 – 60% |
| Dominant transition | hard_cut (~75%), freeze-frames, smash cuts | |
| Dominant emotions | neutral, defiant | |

Wide range because timing varies by joke. Comedy edits often have 0.5s punchline cuts interspersed with 4s setup shots.

## Craft rules

### Core principle: serve the joke
- Every cut exists to either build anticipation or deliver a punchline.
- Setup shots breathe longer (2-4s) to establish expectation.
- Punchline cuts hit hard and short (0.3-0.8s).
- The **contrast** between setup pace and punch pace IS the joke.

### Comic timing devices
- **Freeze frames** at the moment of reaction. Classic comedy editing.
- **Record-scratch sound effect** into a reaction shot. Cliché but effective.
- **Smash cuts** — jarring transition from one tone to another for absurdist effect.
- **Cut away to reaction** — showing someone else's face reacting to what just happened.
- **Extended awkward hold** — the camera staying on someone a beat too long IS the joke.

### Sync approach
- **Internal sync** primarily — the joke lands on a musical accent, not on a beat grid.
- **Beat-sync secondary** — enough to keep musical, not enough to distract.
- **Audio gag sync** — dialogue rips, meme sounds, record scratches carry the music's role in many comedy edits.

### Transitions
- **Hard cuts overwhelming (~75%).** Comedy is about the cut, not the blend.
- **Freeze frames** — functionally a transition from motion to stillness. Common at punchlines.
- **Speed ramps** for slow-mo reaction shots.
- **Flash cuts** sparingly — can read as trying-too-hard.
- **No dissolves** unless for intentional absurdity (e.g. mock-emotional transition in a comedic piece).

### Color + palette
- **Brighter than other types** (~0.42 luma). Natural-lit feel. Comedy needs readability.
- **Moderate saturation** (~0.40). Not aggressive; not muted.
- **Sometimes embrace source-original grade** — mismatched color across sources can itself be a joke.

### Audio
- **Music often secondary.** The song might be a meme song, an unexpectedly serious song over comedic visuals (reverse-tone), or a background bed for dialogue-driven humor.
- **Dialogue rips ESSENTIAL.** Comedy edits lean on iconic lines more than any other type.
- **Meme audio** — the record scratch, the "bruh" vine boom, the MGM lion roar. Treat as expected vocabulary.
- **SFX for gags** — cartoon boings, horns, sad trombones. Overdo them deliberately.

## Structural patterns

Several shapes work:

1. **Setup/punch repeats** — 4-6 beats of [long setup shot] → [short punch cut], different subject each time.
2. **Escalation** — increasingly absurd versions of the same joke. Pacing tightens as absurdity grows.
3. **Reverse-tone** — play seriously, then reveal the joke at the end. Or play comedic, then pull the rug to serious.
4. **Running gag** — single motif that returns with variation throughout.

## When to use this type

- Source material has comedic performances or inherently funny moments.
- Song is either a meme song or playing against tone for humor.
- Goal is to get the viewer to laugh, not to feel.

## Common traps

- **Overusing memes.** One record-scratch hits; three feel lazy.
- **Symmetric pacing.** If every shot is the same length, the joke timing dies. Contrast matters.
- **Trying too hard for effects.** Comedy editing is minimalist at the effect level.
- **Losing the throughline.** Even comedy needs a hook. A random assortment of funny moments isn't an edit, it's a clip reel.

## Why the rules are loose

Community guide verbatim: "few overall guidelines exist for this genre. Intentional rule-breaking is acceptable if it generates laughs." That's the type's defining feature — all the craft principles from other types can be deliberately violated if the violation serves the joke.
