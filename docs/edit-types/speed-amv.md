# Speed AMV / Anime Speed-Edit

Machine-gun pacing. Heavy effects. Impact frames on every downbeat. The TikTok speed-edit aesthetic evolved from anime AMV culture — this is the type where constraints are "break whatever you want."

## Target numbers

| Metric | Target | Range |
|---|---|---|
| Median shot duration | **0.5s** | 0.15 – 1.2s |
| Cuts per minute | **85** | 60 – 120 |
| Beat-sync % | **85%** | 75 – 95% |
| Dominant transition | hard_cut + flash_cut | |
| Dominant emotions | intense | |

Faster than any other type. Shorter duration (30-90s typical, rarely >2min). Higher effect load than any other type.

## Craft rules

### Pacing
- **Sub-second cuts** are the default. 0.3-0.8s dominates. Individual shots lose meaning; the gestalt carries.
- **Impact frames** — brief still frames (1-3 frames) between cuts. Read as flash punctuation.
- **Speed ramps on every third or fourth shot.** Time-stretch the moment of impact.

### Sync approach
- **Beat/cut sync is the foundation.** 85%+. Every downbeat is a cut. Off-beats often cut too.
- **Flash sync** — white flashes on kicks/drops/drop-downs.
- **Lyric sync rare.** At this pacing there's no time to let a lyric land.

### Effects (expected, not optional)
- **Chromatic aberration** on impact frames
- **Light leaks** on zooms
- **Vignettes** pulsing with the beat
- **Frame-stacks** — multiple frames composited briefly for a layered look
- **Zoom punches** — sudden scale-up on a downbeat
- **Scan lines / VHS grain** when intentional retro aesthetic
- **Motion blur** heavily applied

Community guide on AMVs: "multiple simultaneous effects produce novelty; stock effects signal mediocrity." Build custom effect stacks.

### Color + palette
- **Saturation high** (~0.55) — 20 points above action.
- **High contrast** — deep blacks, blown highlights.
- **Aggressive grade per source** — each source can have its own color identity; the speed keeps it from feeling jarring.

### Audio
- **Song choice matters a lot.** Electronic, trap, phonk, hyperpop, drill — anything with a punchy kick and rapid hi-hats. Rock and indie don't work for this pacing.
- **Sub-boom on every drop.** Essential.
- **Bass-boosted song** — speed edits often ship with compressed, pumped audio that reads louder than it is.

## Structure
Not a three-act format. Typical shape:

1. **Cold open** (0-4s) — title card or iconic frame.
2. **Build** (4-15s) — setup, slower initial cuts to establish stakes.
3. **Drop** (15-45s) — the main sequence. Peak pacing.
4. **Breakdown** (45-60s) — brief pause for a callback or quieter moment.
5. **Final drop** (60-80s) — even faster, bigger effects, climax.
6. **Cut to black** — no cooldown, no credits.

Many speed-edits skip the breakdown entirely.

## Common traps

- **Too long.** Speed edits over 90s exhaust viewers. Keep short.
- **Pace inconsistency.** Sudden 3s shots in an 85cpm edit read as bugs. If you want a breather, drop to 20cpm briefly, not mid-range.
- **Effects without purpose.** Every effect should sync to a beat or amplify an impact. Decorative effects flatten the edit.
- **Poor source material.** Speed edits punish low-res or slow-motion source footage. High-motion, well-shot clips work best.

## Reference note

Our corpus is weighted toward action/multifandom, not speed-AMV. The B-tier videos we found hit ~52 cpm; speed-AMV targets 85+. When ingesting future playlists, tag them `speed-amv` to build proper priors — the current corpus shouldn't be used as the pacing reference for this type.
