# Action / Hype

The style Nick is mostly making. Fast, beat-locked, dark-graded, combat-heavy multifandom compilations. What the reference corpus's B-tier edits look like: 0.95s median cut, 52 cpm, 71% on-beat.

## Target numbers

| Metric | Target | Range |
|---|---|---|
| Median shot duration | **1.0s** | 0.5 – 2.0s |
| Cuts per minute | **50** | 40 – 65 |
| Beat-sync % | **70%** | 60 – 80% |
| Dominant transition | hard_cut (~55%) | |
| Dominant emotions | intense, defiant | |

## Craft rules

### Pacing
- Clips last **no more than a second or two**. Community guide (animemusicvideos.org) is explicit: action edits cut faster than anything except speed-AMVs.
- Consistent cut rhythm through most of the edit; reserve slow shots for moments of quiet tension (1-2 per minute at most).
- Downbeats are for the biggest hits; off-beat cuts read as accidents.

### Sync approach
- **Beat/cut sync dominates.** Cuts land exactly on prominent musical notes. This is the style's defining signal.
- **Internal sync layers underneath** — a punch lands on a downbeat, a gunshot on a drop. Even if the cut isn't on the beat, the action within the shot should be.
- **Lyric sync is secondary or absent.** Audiences don't process lyrics on first listen in action edits; don't sacrifice beat-sync to get a lyric match.
- **Flash transitions on beats** highlight sync deliberately — use sparingly, 2-3 per minute max.

### Motion
- **Match cuts across fandoms.** Left-to-right punch thrown from Source A → left-to-right punch landing in Source B. This is the vocabulary.
- **Whip pans** add kinetic chaos; pair them with incoming motion blur.
- **Speed ramps** stretch the moment just before impact, then snap back on the beat.
- Directional movement should stay consistent within a sequence. Jarring direction changes work only for deliberate shock.

### Color + palette
- Dark-saturated grade. 45-55% dark shots is normal for B-tier. Low luma.
- Red/orange fire + blue shadow. Saturation bumped (~0.42) so blood reads, neon pops.
- Consistent color plan across sources — LUT per source helps unify Marvel's bright grade against Mad Max's yellow-teal.

### Audio
- Song drives everything. Song-first, dialogue second.
- Scene audio blended under song at -18 to -22 dB — the crack of a gunshot, the thud of an impact bleeds through.
- SFX layer: sub_boom on every drop, action SFX (punch, kick, gunshot) on the shot where the action occurs. Variant rotation so 5 gunshots don't all sound identical.

## Common traps

- **Too much effect.** White flashes on every beat becomes wallpaper. Use 2-3 per minute, deliberately.
- **Hard-cutting everything.** Top corpus videos land ~52% hard cuts, not 100%. Let match cuts and whip pans do the work.
- **Ignoring the mute test.** Turn off audio. If the visuals don't hold together, the song is carrying dead weight.
