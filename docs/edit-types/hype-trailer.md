# Hype / Trailer

Rising-tension three-act build. Structured like a movie trailer — quiet intro establishing stakes, escalation through the second act, explosive payoff in the third. The pacing accelerates throughout.

## Target numbers

| Metric | Target | Range |
|---|---|---|
| Median shot duration | **2.0s** (average) | 0.5 – 6.0s |
| Cuts per minute | **30** | 20 – 50 (escalating) |
| Beat-sync % | **60%** | 50 – 75% |
| Dominant transition | hard_cut (~55%) + sub-boom impacts | |
| Dominant emotions | hopeful → defiant → intense | |

Single most important rule: **pacing is not constant.** Unlike other types, the pacing DELIBERATELY changes across the three acts.

## Three-act pacing structure

| Act | Duration share | Avg shot duration | Cuts per minute |
|---|---|---|---|
| Act 1 (setup) | 30-40% | **3.0s** | ~20 |
| Act 2 (escalation) | 40-50% | **2.0s** | ~30 |
| Act 3 (climax) | 15-25% | **1.0s** | ~60 |

Drop-timed sub-boom impacts mark each act transition. Title cards often land between acts.

## Craft rules

### Act 1 — Setup
- **Establish stakes.** Who, what, why it matters.
- **Slow cuts** — 2-4s. Let faces and places breathe.
- **Dialogue rips dominate.** Lip-sync to a character's line laying out the central conflict.
- **Color grade slightly desaturated** — the world before the storm.

### Act 2 — Escalation
- **Action ramp.** Cuts tighten to 1.5-2s.
- **Obstacle sequence.** Show every hurdle the character faces.
- **Music typically builds during this act** — buildup sections, risers, rising synth stacks.
- **Color grade warms/saturates** — the world lighting up.

### Act 3 — Climax
- **Pacing explodes.** 0.5-1s cuts. 60+ cpm.
- **Every major beat lands on a musical drop.**
- **Sub-boom SFX on every impact.**
- **Color at peak saturation.**
- **Final shot typically a hero shot** — character landing, standing over victory, iconic silhouette.

### Sync approach
- **Beat-sync moderate (~60%).** Tighter in act 3, looser in act 1.
- **Drop-alignment is critical.** Every song drop must have a corresponding visual climax.
- **Lip-sync in act 1 only.** Don't try to lip-sync in acts 2-3 — there's no space.

### Transitions
- **Hard cuts dominate** (~55%).
- **Sub-boom impacts** on act transitions.
- **Flash cuts** in act 3 on downbeats.
- **Whip pans** during act 2 for kinetic build.
- **Title cards** between acts — "Hero Name" / "Franchise" / "Release Date" style text.
- **Dissolves rare** — maybe one between acts 1 and 2 for emotional bridge.

### Color + palette
- **Trailer-graded** — high contrast, teal-orange Hollywood look.
- **Saturation 0.45** — punchy.
- **Low-ish luma** (~0.28) — trailers trade in darkness and impact.
- **Grade shift per act** — cooler act 1, warmer act 3.

### Audio
- **Song must have structure** — trailer edits NEED a buildup and drop. Pop songs work (ramps), trailer score tracks (2WEI, Audiomachine) work even better.
- **Impact sub-booms** on every major cut in act 3.
- **Voice over** in act 1 — character's "I will do this" speech.
- **Silence before the final drop** — cutting all audio for half a second before the climax is a classic trailer trick.

## Common traps

- **Constant pacing.** If you cut at the same rate for all three acts, you don't have a trailer, you have a montage. Pacing MUST accelerate.
- **No title cards.** Trailers without text feel like amateur compilations. Add at least one title at the act 2 → 3 boundary.
- **Wrong song.** A song without a clear build-and-drop structure breaks the trailer format. Audition songs carefully.
- **Weak act 1.** Editors rush to act 3. Don't. A trailer lives or dies on act 1 — if you don't establish stakes, the climax has no weight.

## When to use this type

- The project has stakes that need to be SET UP before they can be paid off.
- The song has clear buildup/drop structure.
- You want to "sell" the source material to an audience — this is the genre that functions as marketing.
